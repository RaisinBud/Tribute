Phoolan Devi

Indian Bandit and later a Member of Parliament

Phoolan Devi, who later came to be known as Bandit Queen.

Bandit Queen’s struggling journey

Phoolan Devi was born in Uttar Pradesh,India

Phoolan Devi, who later came to be known as Bandit Queen with a movie named after her was born on 10 August 1963 in Ghura Ka Purwa, a small village in Uttar Pradesh. She was the fourth of six children but only she and one older sister survived to adulthood. Phoolan was feisty right since her childhood. When she was 11, her cousin sold their only asset - a neem tree on their small land. Furious Phoolan staged a sit-in on the land and did not give way when the family elders tried to use force to drag them home.

Phoolan Devi’s marriage

Phoolan, at the age of 11 was married to a man in his 30s who raped her days after her wedding. Her deteriorating health forces her parents to take her back home but soon after she rejuvenated energy she started feeling pressure from peers to return back to her husband. She finally got rid of the troublesome marriage after her husband moved in with another woman.

Worst phase of Phoolan Devi’s life

She was gang-raped when she was just 18, by high-caste outlaws after the gang she belonged to was attacked by rivals. She was locked up in Behmai, and for three weeks, she was gang-raped every day.

When Phoolan Devi picked arms

Giving her anger a direction, Phoolan became a gang-leader in her own right to seek revenge. In 1981 Phoolan along with her gang came to village where she had been raped. She recognised two men who raped her and sought whereabouts of the others. When they refused to share details, she rounded up 22 Thakur caste villagers, including two of her rapists.

When Phoolan Devi surrendered to police

After the Behmai incident, media named her as the Bandit Queen and she went absconding for two years compelling the government to negotiate a surrender. Her demands included a land to be given to her, her gang members were not to be sentenced to death but were to offer 8 years of jail and be free thereafter.

When Phoolan Devi’s life turned to politics

She got released from prison in 1996 after 2 years of which she stood for election for Samajwadi Party from the Mirzapur area in Uttar Pradesh and ultimately won the election and served as an MP.

Phoolan Devi’s death

On 25 July 2001, Phoolan was shot dead by three masked gunmen outside of her Delhi bungalow. She was hit five times with three shots to her head and two to her body. She was taken to a nearby hospital but was declared dead. The prime suspect, Sher Singh Rana, allegedly claimed to have murdered Phoolan Devi in revenge for Behmai massacre.
